--------------------------------------------------------------
0. THE ONE THING TO INTERNALISE FIRST
--------------------------------------------------------------
In India, for an unknown D2C store, the bottleneck is NOT design.
It is TRUST and RTO (return-to-origin) losses.

A first-time visitor's real question is never "is this pretty?"
It is: "If I pay, will I actually get the product, and will it be
the real thing and not a cheap fake?"

Every design decision below is judged by one metric: does it move
the buyer from "this might be a scam" to "I'll risk Rs 1,499 on
these guys." Animations and AI images help the VIBE. Trust signals
close the SALE. Do not confuse the two. If you only have budget for
one, build trust first.

This is the honest part most "make it cool" advice skips.


--------------------------------------------------------------
1. WHO IS ACTUALLY BUYING (and why it changes the whole site)
--------------------------------------------------------------
Two people, one purchase:
  - THE PAYER: a parent / uncle / elder cousin. Cares about
    safety, durability, value-for-money, "will it break in 2 days",
    age-appropriateness, and not getting cheated.
  - THE INFLUENCER: the kid. Cares about "it's FAST / it FLIES /
    it looks like a real monster lizard."

So every product needs DUAL messaging:
  - Hero line for the kid: emotion + spectacle ("Drift like a pro.")
  - Spec line for the parent: range, runtime, charge time, age,
    rechargeable, replacement warranty.

Gifting is a massive use-case in India (birthdays, Raksha Bandhan,
Diwali, Children's Day, exam-over rewards). Build "Gift it" framing,
gift wrap option, and "deliver by [date]" promises around festivals.


--------------------------------------------------------------
2. THE HONEST TRUTH ABOUT AI IMAGES (Runware) — READ TWICE
--------------------------------------------------------------
AI images are a weapon for MOOD, and a landmine for PRODUCT.

USE Runware AI for:
  - Hero / banner backgrounds (cinematic, aspirational scenes)
  - Lifestyle scenes (a kid flying a drone in a park at golden hour,
    an RC car kicking up dust on a track)
  - Category tile art, festival campaign creatives, email headers
  - Abstract motion / particle backgrounds behind text
  - 404 / empty-state art, loading illustrations

DO NOT use AI for:
  - The actual product image on the product card or product page.
  - Spec-accurate shots (the customer must see EXACTLY what arrives).

Why this matters (the part that costs real money): if the AI hero
shows a sleek matte drone with a 4K gimbal and the box contains a
shiny plastic toy, you get returns, COD refusals, 1-star reviews,
and RTO shipping losses BOTH ways. In India RTO can quietly eat
your entire margin. AI-perfect product shots are the #1 self-inflicted
wound for toy/electronics D2C. Real photos / real video for the
product. AI for the world around it.

Rule of thumb: AI sells the DREAM in the hero; REAL MEDIA keeps the
PROMISE on the product page.

RUNWARE SETUP NOTE: your key is not in the project yet. Add
RUNWARE_API_KEY to .env.local and to Vercel env vars. Generate
images in a build/admin script and store the OUTPUT files in the
repo or Supabase Storage — never call the paid API on every page
view. Generate once, serve as static optimised next/image.

Concrete Runware prompts to start with (1536x640 hero ratio):
  - "Cinematic wide shot, a remote-control rally car drifting on a
     dusty desert track at golden hour, motion blur, dramatic low
     sun, dust trail, shallow depth of field, ultra detailed, no
     text"
  - "A child in a sunny Indian park flying a small white quadcopter
     drone, blue sky, soft bokeh, joyful, cinematic, golden hour"
  - "Macro studio shot, glossy robotic lizard toy on dark reflective
     surface, neon rim light, dramatic shadows, product mood, no text"
Negative prompt always: "text, watermark, logo, distorted hands,
extra limbs, blurry, low quality".


--------------------------------------------------------------
3. THE TRUST STACK (this is what actually lifts conversion in India)
--------------------------------------------------------------
Put these EVERYWHERE they're relevant, not buried in a footer:

  a) CASH ON DELIVERY badge. This single feature can double
     conversion for a new Indian store. Show "COD available" on
     the product card AND the cart. (Pair with anti-RTO below.)
  b) WhatsApp support button (floating). Indians want to chat
     before paying. A real human reply on WhatsApp closes sales
     no chatbot will. Use the official wa.me link or WhatsApp
     Business API.
  c) Real, visible phone number + a real city address + GST number
     in the footer and on /contact. Anonymous = scam in the buyer's
     mind.
  d) Clear, generous, plainly-worded return/replacement policy:
     "7-day replacement if damaged or not working." Say it on the
     product page, not just /returns.
  e) Razorpay / "100% Secure Payments" trust strip with UPI, cards,
     RuPay, COD logos near the buy button.
  f) Real reviews WITH photos/video from real buyers. Indians trust
     "video unboxing by a real person" more than star ratings.
     NEVER fake reviews — one exposed fake kills the brand. Seed
     honestly: ask early buyers for a review in exchange for a small
     coupon, clearly labelled.
  g) Delivery ETA by pincode ("Deliver to 110001 by Tue, 12 Jun").
     Uncertainty about delivery time kills carts.
  h) Stock/urgency that is TRUE ("Only 4 left") — never fake
     countdowns. Buyers smell fake urgency and it destroys trust.
  i) "Genuine product / Brand warranty" and "Indian-voltage charger
     included" notes for anything that charges.

Honest note: items (a), (b), (g) move the needle more than any
animation on this list. If you do nothing else, do those three.


--------------------------------------------------------------
4. THE PRODUCT PAGE (PDP) — where the sale is won or lost
--------------------------------------------------------------
Order, top to bottom, mobile-first:

  1. PRODUCT-IN-ACTION VIDEO as the first media (autoplay muted,
     loop, with a poster image). For RC, the buyer MUST see it move.
     A 6-10s clip of the car drifting / drone hovering / lizard
     scuttling converts far better than any still. This is the most
     important single asset on the whole site.
  2. Title + dual line (spectacle line + 1-line spec summary).
  3. Price block: big price, MRP strikethrough, "X% OFF", COD badge,
     "Deliver by [date]" with pincode check.
  4. Sticky "Add to Cart" / "Buy Now" on mobile (always visible).
  5. SPEC CHIPS, scannable (this is electronics — specs sell):
       Range: 30m  ·  Runtime: 20 min  ·  Charge: 90 min  ·
       Age: 6+  ·  Rechargeable Li-ion  ·  2.4GHz
  6. "What's in the box" with icons (car, controller, USB charger,
     spare tyres, manual). Reduces "will I need to buy batteries?"
     anxiety.
  7. Trust strip: 7-day replacement · Secure payment · COD.
  8. Reviews with photos/video.
  9. "Frequently bought together" / spare parts / extra battery
     (huge AOV lever for RC — batteries, spare wheels, propellers).
 10. FAQ block (charging, range, age, spare availability).

The spec chips + the action video are non-negotiable for electronics.


--------------------------------------------------------------
5. ANIMATIONS & VIDEO — what to use, and the honest performance cost
--------------------------------------------------------------
Animation should reward attention and give feedback, never block the
content or slow the page. ~80% of your traffic is mid-range Android
on variable networks. A heavy hero video that delays load will LOSE
more sales than the animation gains. Budget ruthlessly.

WHAT TO BUILD (in priority order):

  A) HERO BACKGROUND VIDEO LOOP (the showpiece)
     - A short 6-10s muted loop: RC car drifting / drone rising.
     - MUST have: poster image (instant paint), preload="none" until
       in view, <= ~2-3 MB, MP4 + WebM, autoplay muted playsinline,
       and a static AI image fallback on slow connections / reduced
       motion. Lazy-load it; never let it block LCP.
     - This is where Runware can also generate a looping ambient
       video / you composite real product clips over an AI scene.

  B) PRODUCT-IN-ACTION CLIPS on each PDP (see section 4).

  C) MICRO-INTERACTIONS (cheap, CSS/transform only, big polish/$):
     - Add-to-cart: button morphs to a check + cart count bumps.
     - Card hover: slight lift + product image parallax/tilt.
     - Wishlist heart: fill + tiny pop.
     - Scroll-reveal: sections fade-up as they enter (you already
       have .reveal — reuse it).
     - "Fly-to-cart" arc when adding (delightful for a toy brand).

  D) A 30-60s BRAND / EXPLAINER VIDEO on the homepage + /about
     ("Who we are, where we ship from, our replacement promise").
     A founder's face talking to camera builds more trust than any
     graphic. Put a real human on screen.

  E) CATEGORY TILES with subtle perpetual motion (a drone gently
     bobbing, wheels with a slow spin on hover).

HARD RULES (the honest engineering truth):
  - Respect prefers-reduced-motion (you already guard this — keep it).
  - Never autoplay video with sound. Ever.
  - All decorative motion = transform/opacity only (GPU-cheap).
  - Animation must NEVER delay first paint or LCP. Poster images
    first, video second.
  - Test on a real ~Rs 12k Android phone on 4G, not your laptop.
  - If an animation doesn't either (a) give feedback or (b) sell the
    product moving, cut it.

--------------------------------------------------------------
  1. Top utility bar: free shipping threshold, COD, support hours.
  2. Hero: video loop of an RC car/drone in action + one bold
     promise + "Shop RC Cars" / "Shop Drones" CTAs + a small trust
     row (COD · 7-day replacement · Secure pay).
  3. Shop by category: RC Cars, Drones, RC Planes, Helicopters,
     RC Animals (lizard/snake/dino), Boats, Spare Parts & Batteries.
  4. "Best sellers / Trending" rail with action thumbnails.
  5. A trust band: "Why buy from us" (real photos: your team, your
     packaging, your replacement promise) — NOT stock icons.
  6. Deal of the day / festival banner (true countdown only).
  7. Real review wall (photos/video from buyers).
  8. Brand explainer video + newsletter/WhatsApp opt-in.
  9. Footer: address, GST, phone, policies, payment logos.


--------------------------------------------------------------
7. CATEGORIES & MERCHANDISING SPECIFIC TO RC/ELECTRONIC TOYS
--------------------------------------------------------------
  - RC Cars: split by type (Rock Crawler, Drift, Stunt/Flip, Monster
    Truck, High-Speed). Filter by speed, scale (1:10/1:16), terrain.
  - Drones: BY WEIGHT FIRST (see legal note section 9), then by
    camera/no-camera, flight time, range.
  - RC Animals: lean into the "prank / scare" angle — these sell on
    novelty and short funny videos. Great for organic social reach.
  - Spare parts & batteries: this is your repeat-purchase and AOV
    engine. Make spares findable from the product page. An RC buyer
    who can easily get a spare battery becomes a loyal customer.
  - Filters that matter for electronics: age, price, battery life,
    range, rechargeable vs AA, indoor/outdoor, skill level.


--------------------------------------------------------------
8. THE RTO / FAKE-COD PROBLEM (the silent profit killer)
--------------------------------------------------------------
COD lifts conversion but invites fake/curiosity orders that get
refused on delivery — you eat 2-way shipping. Mitigate WITHOUT
killing COD:
  - Offer a small discount / free shipping for PREPAID (UPI) to nudge
    prepaid: "Pay online & save Rs 50."
  - OTP-confirm COD orders (you already have Firebase phone OTP —
    reuse it to verify the number before confirming COD).
  - Flag/limit COD on high-value items or repeat-refuser numbers.
  - Clear delivery-date expectations to reduce "changed my mind".
This is unglamorous but it's the difference between a store that
survives and one that bleeds out quietly.


--------------------------------------------------------------
9. LEGAL / COMPLIANCE YOU MUST NOT IGNORE (honest, often missed)
--------------------------------------------------------------
  - DRONES IN INDIA: DGCA rules apply. "Nano" drones (<= 250g, low
    speed/height) are largely exempt from registration; heavier
    drones need a UIN registered on the Digital Sky portal. For a
    toy store, STOCK MOSTLY SUB-250G TOY DRONES and clearly state
    the weight + "no registration required for nano category" on the
    PDP. For anything heavier, add a buyer advisory. Getting this
    wrong is a real liability, not a nicety.
  - BIS / safety: toys sold in India fall under quality control
    orders; sell BIS-compliant stock and keep proof.
  - Lithium battery shipping: some couriers restrict Li-ion. Confirm
    with your courier; label correctly.
  - GST invoice on every order (buyers gifting / claiming expect it).
  - Clear age warnings + small-parts choking warning for under-3.
None of this is optional if you want to scale without a takedown.


--------------------------------------------------------------
10. TECH NOTES THAT FIT YOUR EXISTING STACK
--------------------------------------------------------------
  - Keep Next.js App Router + Tailwind. It's the right tool here.
  - Images: next/image everywhere; AI assets generated ONCE via
    Runware in a script, stored static or in Supabase Storage, then
    served optimised. Never per-request paid calls.
  - Video: self-host short loops on Vercel/Supabase or a CDN; MP4+WebM,
    poster images, lazy. Consider a cheap video CDN if traffic grows.
  - Supabase: add tables/columns for specs (range, runtime, charge,
    weight_grams, age_min), media (video_url, gallery), stock count,
    and reviews-with-media (photo_url/video_url on the reviews table).
  - Pincode ETA: start with a simple static pincode->zone->days map;
    upgrade to a courier API later.
  - WhatsApp: start with a wa.me floating button; upgrade to Business
    API for order updates.
  - Performance budget: LCP < 2.5s on 4G mobile. Treat it as a
    release gate. A beautiful slow site converts worse than a plain
    fast one.

If you gave me one week, in order:

Notice: the "cool animations" you asked about are step 5-6, NOT
step 1. That ordering IS the honest answer. Make it trustworthy and
fast first, then make it dazzle. A dazzling store nobody trusts
sells nothing; a plain store people trust sells every day.


--------------------------------------------------------------
12. BLUNT SUMMARY
--------------------------------------------------------------
  - Trust > beauty for a new Indian D2C store. Build COD, WhatsApp,
    real identity, real reviews, pincode ETA first.
  - AI (Runware) for mood/hero/lifestyle. Real photo + real video for
    the actual product. Mixing them up causes returns and kills you.
  - The product-in-action VIDEO is the single highest-leverage asset.
  - Animations: feedback + spectacle only, transform/opacity, lazy,
    reduced-motion safe, never block load. Test on a cheap Android.
  - Handle RTO (COD OTP + prepaid nudge) or it eats your margin.
  - Drones = real legal compliance (DGCA / sub-250g). Don't wing it.
  - Make it fast and trustworthy, THEN make it beautiful.

That's exactly what I'd do if I were in your place.
